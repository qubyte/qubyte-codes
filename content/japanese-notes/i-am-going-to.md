A combination of the volitional form of a verb and r「〜と、、思、おも、います、」.

For example:

- I (have just decided I) will go to the park today.
- <span lang="ja">r「今日、きょう」、r「公、こう、園、えん」にr「行、い」こうとr「思、おも」います。</span>

The literal translation is "I think I shall go to the park today."

The present tense comes with the nuance that the speaker has just made a
decision.

The sentence can be modified to the present progressive to indicate that the
decision is not new.

- I (decided earlier that I) will go to the park today.
- <span lang="ja">r「今日、きょう」、r「公、こう、園、えん」にr「行、い」こうとr「思、おも」っています。</span>
